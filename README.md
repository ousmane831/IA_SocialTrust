Across many developing and emerging countries, access to social assistance programs remains a critical challenge. Citizens often face opaque decision-making processes when applying for public aid such as healthcare coverage, financial support, or family assistance programs. These systems frequently lack transparency, making it difficult for applicants to understand why they are accepted or rejected.

To address this issue, we propose **SunuAISocialTrust**, an AI-powered platform designed to ensure fair, transparent, and explainable decision-making in social assistance programs.

The platform allows citizens to select a specific program—such as healthcare support, financial aid, or family assistance—and submit their application through a simple and accessible interface. Based on the selected program, the system dynamically adapts the form to collect only relevant information, improving usability and reducing complexity.

Our AI model evaluates eligibility using socio-economic data and provides not only a decision but also a clear and understandable explanation. Instead of technical outputs, users receive simple insights such as “your income level exceeds the threshold” or “your household size increases your eligibility,” making the system accessible even to users with low digital literacy.

To ensure fairness, the platform integrates bias detection mechanisms that monitor potential discrimination based on sensitive factors such as gender or region. If a risk of bias is detected, the system flags the decision for review.

Additionally, SunuAISocialTrust includes a built-in appeal system. If a citizen is not eligible, the platform automatically suggests steps to contest the decision, ensuring accountability and inclusion.

Security and trust are central to our approach. All user data is protected using strong encryption, guaranteeing privacy and safe handling of sensitive information.

By combining transparency, fairness, and accessibility, SunuAISocialTrust empowers citizens, strengthens trust in public institutions, and promotes inclusive access to social services. The solution is scalable and can be adapted to different countries and social programs, making it a powerful tool for building trustworthy AI systems worldwide.
